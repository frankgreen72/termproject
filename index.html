Context

From a history of urban renewal and relentless land pressure, Chinatown today is ever-so a site of contention. Indeed, if Chinatown now functions as an incomplete community, it is largely due to its extensive history of planning insensitivity and development leniency, which allowed the incremental suppression of essential services and amenities - notably carved out of the urban fabric through several large redevelopment projects (e.g., Complexe Guy-Favreau). This has led to the demolition of many housing blocks, local businesses, groceries, parks, and religious buildings - never to be replaced.

As such, land pressure in the area has reached its pinnacle, hemming the local community to the point of suffocation. In an environment where every parcel of land is fiercely contested, planning for the provision of services is of critical importance.
